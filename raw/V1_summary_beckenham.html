The Borough of Beckenham suffered very badly from V1 attacks
and to a lesser extent from V2's. In all 69 V1's and 5 V2's are
recorded  in official records.  About 138 people died  and there
was major destruction to housing and other buildings  
Beckenham suffered several  serious incidents : 44 died at
Clockhouse, 18 at Elmers End bus garage and 12 at Fairfield
Road. In addition to this there were numerous domestic incidents
which are listed in the log.
               Beckenham
Fairfield Road Beckenham after the Flying bomb 5th January 1945. Note that the surface brick built air raid shelters have
stood up well to the blast. Christ Church behind was badly damaged but must have been repaired fairly quickly after the
war as my Aunt was married there in 1952.
The recent was taken in 2004 and shows that much of the site has remained undeveloped and it used as a car park. The
Church has been repaired but the signs of re-building are clear to see. The rows of housing on the left and right were once
terraced and have now become semis.

Some areas of Beckenham,attracted V1's again and again. The Queens Road area had 5  and the Cator
Park area 5 V1's and 2 V'2's

In one respect Beckenham reversed the trend of  other boroughs in that the V2's caused less casualties
per incident than the V1's. Several Of the V2's fell on sports grounds and one in an area of low density
housing in Eden  Park The only V2 that caused fatalities was the Crystal Palace Park Road incident
where 9 died.
