Croydon CR7 Thornton Heath, was badly battered during the
V1 attacks. In total 15 Flying Bombs exploded in this fairly
small district killing about 61 people.
Thornton Heaths worst tragedy was on 1st July 1944 09.56
when a Flying Bomb exploded in Nursery Road. It killed 9
people and demolished 14 houses.

There are several aspects of the Thornton Heath V bombings that stand out;
Firstly, of the total of 15  V1's, 10 struck in the 5 days between 1st and 5th of July 1944 Although it can
only be conjecture, it would seem  likely,  all the missiles were launched from one site in France.
It is also interesting the first missile did not fall until 1st July, most districts received impacts in the last
two weeks of June.  This could possibly point to the range of the launch site in question being reduced  
because of  false intelligence Britain fed the Nazis  the V1's were overshooting.

A further point about Thornton's Heaths suffering is the high death rate per missile, 4.07. Thornton
Heath is an outer London Suburb and equivalent areas such as Streatham or Upper Norwood had a
much lower (1.88 and 1.00 respectively). The key to this is the nature of the housing of the area. Whilst
much of Upper Norwood and Streatham had larger houses and areas of open space Thornton Heath was
more tightly packed with small terraced houses.  In Thornton Heath 80% of the V1's resulted in fatalities
whereas in South London as a whole only 50% were fatal incidents.
